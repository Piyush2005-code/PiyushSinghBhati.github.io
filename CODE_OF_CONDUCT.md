# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We are committed to providing a welcoming and inspiring community for all. We pledge that everyone participating in the AI Robotics Visionary community—including code, issues, discussions, and all other forms of communication—will treat one another with respect and dignity.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to creating a positive environment include:

- **Being respectful** of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
- **Using welcoming and inclusive language**
- Focusing on **constructive criticism** and avoiding personal attacks
- Being **patient and understanding** with others
- Showing **empathy** toward other community members
- Being **open to feedback** and willing to improve
- Acknowledging and **celebrating diverse perspectives**
- **Helping others** grow and learn from one another

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment, discrimination, or intimidation of any kind
- Offensive comments related to gender, gender identity and expression, sexual orientation, disability, mental illness, neuro(a)typicality, physical appearance, body size, age, race, or religion
- Unwanted sexual attention or advances
- Threats of violence or violent language directed against another person
- Deliberate intimidation or stalking
- Publishing others' private information without consent ("doxing")
- Sustained disruption of community spaces
- Advocating for, or encouraging, any of the above behaviors

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders, including maintainers and moderators, are responsible for:

- Clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior
- Taking appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful
- Removing, editing, or rejecting contributions, messages, or comments that violate this Code of Conduct
- Banning individuals who violate this Code of Conduct, either temporarily or permanently

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- All community spaces, including GitHub repositories, issues, pull requests, and discussions
- All forms of communication (comments, issues, pull requests, private messages, etc.)
- Interactions that occur on behalf of the community (at events, conferences, etc.)

## Reporting a Violation

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, or have any other concerns, please report it by emailing the project maintainers. All reports will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

**Email**: [maintainer email or contact form]

When reporting, please provide:
- Your contact information
- Names/identities of those involved
- Description of what happened
- Any relevant context or evidence
- Whether this is an ongoing situation

All reports will be kept confidential. We will not disclose the reporter's identity without their consent, except as necessary to address the situation.

## Response and Consequences

Depending on the severity and frequency of violations, actions may include:

1. **Private warning** - A respectful conversation about the violation
2. **Public warning** - A notice that the behavior is unacceptable
3. **Temporary ban** - Removal from the community for a specified period
4. **Permanent ban** - Removal from the community indefinitely
5. **Legal action** - In cases of serious violations like threats or harassment

## Appeals

If you believe you have been unfairly sanctioned, you may appeal to the project maintainers within 30 days of the action. Appeals should include:

- Your name and contact information
- The action taken against you
- Reason for your appeal
- Any additional context
